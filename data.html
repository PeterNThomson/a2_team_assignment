Skills Summary

In terms of IT specific skills, disregarding a degree and industry experience which are considered as desirable across almost all IT jobs. Some of these skills required such as Unity engine, LabView Programming and TypeScript is considered more niche, not making an appearance on the top skills list at all. Windows, networking, object-oriented programming skills, cloud-based abilities and the C family of coding languages on the other hand are quite high on this list.
It is unsurprising that communication skills are (by an incredible margin) at the top of the list in terms of general skills, along with all non-IT specific skills making an appearance on the list in some way, shape or form.


Three IT related skills that are in demand but not present, or at least not specifically outlined under our various desired job listings are SQL, Java and JavaScript.
When looking at non-IT related skills, we would be hesitant to list anything here as every single skill on this list, from communication skills, all the way down to decision making (which is at the bottom of the list compared to communication being at the top) are, in our minds, all essential skills. It wouldn't really be feasible for a company to list all of these non-essential skills on every job listing, and sure there is bound to be outlying examples where you may not be required for example, to have amazing presentation skills. But in general, it is our belief that all non-essential IT skills would be highly sort after in any industry, not just IT.
