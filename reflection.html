Group Reflection

Alexander
My experience working with the other Procrastinators has been a blast. The team environment is fun and relaxed while still encouraging us to get work done, and my teammates are supportive and friendly. Most of us are accustomed to working independently, so I was surprised by how easy it was to coordinate and complete tasks despite our lack of experience working in a group setting.
One thing that could be improved is our communication in and outside meetings. This issue probably stems from our habit of working alone and lack of reliance on others, but this didn’t bother me personally. I felt confident that we would all complete our tasks and enjoyed seeing the finished work popping up on our website like magic.

Working with a team as good as this one has opened my eyes to the unique experiences that being with others can bring to the table. It’s great hearing everyone’s input on ideas and fascinating to learn about each other as people. We’re only halfway through our journey, and it can only get better from here. I imagine after everything is said and done, many of us will keep in touch and even become good friends.

For me, the highlight of assessment 2 was the amazing team of like-minded individuals I was able to work and continue my IT studies with, as there is a very happy and friendly flow between the group, not to mention the key aspects in which we are all perusing common goals! We are all so passionate about what we are doing which makes for an easy flowing work environment, along with a fun one. In the future I wish to put 110% percent into this, as opposed to just 100%. You have not seen the last of us yet! "The Procrastinator's" will keep shining on!

Peter
The highlight of this assignment for me was meeting a group of awesome, like-minded guys who are as passionate as I am for the IT industry. An improvement moving forward with the rest of the course, in my eyes, would be our usage of Microsoft Teams. I feel like portions of our meetings weren't really achieving a whole lot and I think going into future meetings having a more clearly defined agenda, and better recording of actions discussed would help us keep the meetings to a more reasonable time limit as a couple of them were a little drawn out. While I wouldn't say that it was necessarily surprising, but more just interesting, was hearing/reading about the groups different backgrounds and what drove them to seek a career in the IT industry, particularly given that we all had fairly similar job title goals in mind, yet for vastly different companies. As someone who has been working in a quite specific type of team environment for the better part of 15 years as a chef, something that become clear to me through this assignment was the difference in pacing. Working in a more relaxed and thought driven scenario was a welcome change to the rush and volume of a kitchen.
